School Project 2025

by Carol Kibukamusoke and Nathan Wasolo

This year, in true KPS spirit, we have come together yet again to give back to our school.

It all started with a visit to the School on September 25, 2025 by Nathan Wasolo and Carol Kibukamusoke.

The Headmistress and her Deputy expressed their gratitude for our continued interest in partnering with the school administration.

Our representatives together with the Headmistress and her Deputy agreed that the focus for this year be to mobilise resources for textbooks for the upper primary section of our old school, Kitante Primary School(KPS) and planting trees.

Nathan and Carol meeting with the Headmistress and her Deputy on their initial viist.

Prior to the handover ceremony, Mr. Kirya read out a concept note prepared by the school administration for the old students, in which is highlighted the current academic and institutional status of the school,

key achievements in staff development and improvements to the school infrastructure and the existing challenges affecting academic performance and the school's development.

It is clear that our continued involvement and collaboration is needed in order for the school to continue to deliver quality education to the learners.

Presentation of the concept note by the school administration; a copy was shared on the group.

On November 15, 2025 a group of us joined Carol and Nathan at the school and participated in the handing over of the text books and symbolic transplanting of 10no trees.

Atleast 90 textbooks of varying titles and quantities as per a list compiled by the Deputy H/M have been donated and 150no. tree seedlings transplanted.

Anita, our treasurer, has submitted the final amount collected for this project as UGX. 3,184,596/-, (over and above what was required), and we thank all who were able to make a contribution to this cause.

The headmistress joined us in a short tour of the school which was a trip down memory lane as many identified their old classrooms, remembered their class teachers and other interesting things about our childhood.

The tour provided an opportunity for the school administration team to show us the staff accomodation block that is under construction, 1no. storey down, 2no. more to go.

They also showed us the renovated classrooms through the support of parents and other partners, as well as the pending renovations particularly the roofing of some classroom blocks.

We ended the tour by planting the remaining 10no. tree seedlings at locations previously identified by the school

After the planting we were treated to a sumptuous meal, before we dispersed after a fulfilling afternoon.

A short video compilation of all who planted a tree.Time will tell us who has a green thumb!
